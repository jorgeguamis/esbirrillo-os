# Life Map Framework

This framework is adapted from Alex Lieberman's "Life Map" — a simple model for evaluating whether your life is balanced and aligned with your values.

The premise: success in one area while failing in others is not success. This framework helps you see the whole picture.

---

## The Six Pillars

Your life can be evaluated across six core areas:

1. **Career** — Your work, business, professional growth, and impact
2. **Relationships** — Partner, family, friendships, community
3. **Health** — Physical fitness, nutrition, sleep, energy, longevity
4. **Meaning** — Purpose, values, contribution, spirituality or philosophy
5. **Finances** — Income, savings, net worth, financial security
6. **Fun** — Play, hobbies, adventure, joy, experiences

---

## Instructions

For each pillar:
1. Rate your current state (1-10)
2. Describe what's working and what's not
3. Define what a "10" would look like
4. Identify one action to improve this area

Then step back and look at the whole map. Where's the imbalance? What's the priority?

## Part 3: Strategic Decisions

### The Imbalance Question

Most high-performers are imbalanced. That's normal. The question is: **Is the imbalance intentional or by default?**

**Are you consciously choosing to prioritize career over relationships right now?**
- If yes, for how long? What's the end date?
- If no, what needs to change?

**Are you sacrificing health for financial growth?**
- If yes, is that sustainable? What's the exit plan?
- If no, what's causing the drift?

## Notes

**This is not about perfection.**
You will never score 10/10 on all six pillars simultaneously. That's not the goal.

**This is about awareness.**
The worst imbalance is the one you don't see.

**This is about intention.**
If you're going to sacrifice health for career growth, make that choice consciously, with a time limit and an exit plan. Don't drift into it.

**This is about alignment.**
Your Life Map should reflect your values. If you say you value family but score relationships at 3/10 for three years straight, something's misaligned.

Do this exercise every quarter. It takes 20 minutes and prevents years of regret.
