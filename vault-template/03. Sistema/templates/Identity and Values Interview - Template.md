# Identity and Values Interview

This interview helps you clarify who you are, what you stand for, and what you will not compromise on.

Most people inherit their values. This interview asks you to examine yours consciously.

Set aside 60 minutes. Answer honestly. This is foundational work.

## Notes

Your values are not aspirations. They're revealed by your choices.

If you say you value family but work 80-hour weeks and miss bedtime every night, you don't actually value family — you value career. Own it.

If you say you value integrity but cut corners when no one's watching, you value results over integrity. Acknowledge it.

The point of this interview is not to judge yourself. It's to see yourself clearly.

Once you see clearly, you can decide: do I want to change my values, or change my behavior?

Either is fine. Just don't lie to yourself.
