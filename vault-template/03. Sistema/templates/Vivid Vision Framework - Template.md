# Vivid Vision Framework

This framework is inspired by Tony Robbins and Cameron Herold's approach to future visioning.

The idea: describe your ideal future in such vivid detail that it becomes real before it happens.

This is not a business plan. This is a description of your life and work 3 years from now, written as if it's already true.

---

## Instructions

1. Set a date exactly 3 years from today: **[DATE 3 YEARS OUT]**
2. Imagine it's that date. Everything you wanted to create has happened. You succeeded.
3. Write in present tense, as if you're living it now.
4. Be specific. Not "I'm successful" — but "I wake up at 6am in my home in [location], spend an hour [doing what], then head to [where]."
5. Include sensory details. What do you see, hear, feel?
6. Cover all areas: work, relationships, health, finances, environment, daily routine.
7. Aim for 1000-2000 words. This should feel real.

## Your Vivid Vision: 3 Years from Today

**Date:** [3 YEARS FROM NOW]

### Morning Routine

It's [day of week], and I wake up at [time]...

[Describe your morning in detail. Where are you? Who's next to you? What's the first thing you do? How do you feel physically? What's your energy level? What do you eat? Who do you talk to?]

---

### Work and Business

My company, [YOUR COMPANY], is now...

[Describe the state of your business. How big is the team? What's the revenue? What do customers say about you? What's your role day-to-day? How many hours do you work? What does a typical work day look like? What problems have you solved? What's your reputation in the industry?]

---

### Your Role and Responsibilities

I spend my time...

[What do you actually do? Are you still in the weeds, or have you transitioned? What meetings do you attend? What decisions do you make? What have you delegated? What fires do you still put out, if any?]

---

### Team and Culture

The people I work with...

[Who's on your leadership team? What's the culture like? How do people talk about working with you? What's your relationship with your co-founders or key partners? How do decisions get made?]

---

### Financial Reality

My financial situation is...

[What's your net worth? What's your annual income? How much cash do you have? What does financial security feel like now? What money worries are gone? What money decisions are easy now?]

---

### Relationships and Family

My relationship with [partner/family/kids] is...

[How much time do you spend with them? What do you do together? How do they describe you? What's different about these relationships compared to 3 years ago? What conflicts have you resolved?]

---

### Health and Energy

Physically, I...

[What's your weight, fitness level, energy? What do you eat? How often do you exercise? What does your body feel like? What health issues have you resolved? How do you feel when you wake up?]

---

### Daily Environment

I live in...

[Where's your home? What does it look like? What do you see when you look out the window? What's your workspace like? What objects are around you? How does your environment make you feel?]

---

### How You Spend Your Time

A typical week looks like...

[Break down your week. How many hours on work? Family? Health? Learning? Recreation? What's your balance? What do you do on weekends? What do you never do anymore?]

---

### What You're Known For

People describe me as...

[What's your reputation? What do people say when you leave the room? What are you known for in your industry? In your personal life? What do people come to you for?]

---

### What You've Let Go Of

I no longer...

[What have you stopped doing? What worries are gone? What responsibilities have you delegated or eliminated? What relationships have you ended? What do you no longer tolerate?]

---

### What Excites You

I wake up excited about...

[What pulls you forward? What projects, relationships, or challenges energize you? What are you building toward next? What feels possible now that didn't before?]

---

### A Typical Tuesday

It's a random Tuesday. Here's what happens...

[Walk through a full day, hour by hour. Wake up, morning routine, work, meals, conversations, evening, bedtime. Make it real. Make it specific.]

---

### The Feeling

The dominant feeling in my life is...

[One word, then explain it. Is it calm? Energized? Proud? Free? Purposeful? Challenged? Expansive?]

## How to Use This Vision

1. **Read it monthly.** Does it still excite you? If not, update it.
2. **Reference it in quarterly reviews.** Are your current actions moving you toward this vision, or away from it?
3. **Share it selectively.** With your partner. With a trusted advisor. With your co-founder. Not with everyone.
4. **Let it evolve.** Your vision will change as you change. That's expected.
5. **Notice the gap.** The difference between this vision and your current reality is your roadmap.

## Notes

This exercise feels indulgent. It's not.

You're a CEO. You make decisions daily that compound into years. If you don't know where you're going, you'll end up somewhere by default.

This vision is not a rigid plan. It's a North Star. It helps you make decisions.

When someone offers you an opportunity, ask: does this move me toward my Vivid Vision, or away from it?

When you're deciding how to spend your time, ask: does this build the life I described, or distract from it?

Clarity compounds. This is worth the time.
